Bank Marketing Data Set

Input variables:
# bank client data:
1 - age (numeric)

2 - job : type of job (categorical: 'admin.'=0,'blue-collar'=1,'entrepreneur'=2,'housemaid'=3,'management'=4,'retired'=5,'self-employed'=6,'services'=7,'student'=8,'technician'=9,'unemployed'=10,'unknown'=11)

3 - marital : marital status (categorical: 'divorced'=0,'married'=1,'single'=2,'unknown'=3; note: 'divorced' means divorced or widowed)

4 - education (categorical: 'basic.4y'=0,'basic.6y'=1,'basic.9y'=2,'high.school'=3,'illiterate'=4,'professional.course'=5,'university.degree'=6,'unknown'=7)

5 - default: has credit in default? (categorical: 'no'=0,'yes'=1,'unknown'=2)

6 - housing: has housing loan? (categorical: 'no'=0,'yes'=1,'unknown'=2)

7 - loan: has personal loan? (categorical: 'no'=0,'yes'=1,'unknown'=2)

8 - contact: contact communication type (categorical: 'cellular'=0,'telephone'=1)

9 - month: last contact month of year (categorical: 'jan'=1, 'feb'=2, 'mar'=3, ..., 'nov'=11, 'dec'=12)

10 - day_of_week: last contact day of the week (categorical: 'mon'=1,'tue'=2,'wed'=3,'thu'=4,'fri'=5)

15 - poutcome: outcome of the previous marketing campaign (categorical: 'failure'=0,'nonexistent'=1,'success'=2)
# social and economic context attributes

21 - y - has the client subscribed a term deposit? (binary: 'yes'=1,'no'=0)
